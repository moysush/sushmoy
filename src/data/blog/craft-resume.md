When I decided to switch careers into **software development**, I didn't expect the resume to be the hard part. Turns out, finding the right tool to build one was its own rabbit hole. In this post, I'll share what the journey looked like and what I discovered.

> There's no perfect resume.

You can view the final output of my resume [here](https://rxresu.me/sushmoy/mahmud-hossain-sushmoy-resume).

## Path of the Resume 🛣️

For my upcoming job hunt, I had to prepare my resume, and it's been a wild ride. I've tried dozens of resume builders online over the years, including Novoresume, OpenResume, Teal, etc. Most of them felt limiting in some ways unless I used their paid service (not OpenResume, though). OpenResume is great, but it only offers a few options, and I found no way to add the functionalities I need there. You may ask, why not just use plain documents? Well, I've tried it too, it can become quite messy and hard to maintain in the long run, because having full control over every design decision is exactly what makes it hard to stay consistent.

## Settling 🏠

After a while of searching, I stumbled upon [Reactive Resume](https://rxresu.me/), which is an open-source resume builder that helps you build your resume any way you like. You can even just write a JSON file and dump your data there in an organized way, and the builder will show your resume perfectly in any theme you prefer. Reactive Resume provides just enough functionality to let you design your resume in "your way" without breaking the consistency in structure. They also let you store several versions of the resume in your account and have a public share link.

## Customization 🎨

### Organization

There are various ways you can organize the sections in your resume. But you should know which is more suitable for your next job hunt. For example, as I'm new to the field and don't have professional work experience yet, I used the following format to organize the sections:

```
Name -> Title (for the position) -> Contact -> Objective Summary -> Skills -> Projects -> Education & Certifications
```

If you have experiences in your field, you may add the "Work Experiences" section before mentioning your projects, and you can even eliminate the projects section if you have enough experiences.

### Template

I used the default **Onyx** template from Reactive Resume, as it looked very simple, which satisfied my need. For the primary accent color, I used a **deep teal** color (`#0891b2`) to have a minimal look. I also used a slightly **soft dark** color (`#1f2937`) for the texts.

### The Chosen Fonts ⚡

Fonts are one of the main ingredients of your resume. For the headers, I used **Inter** for that modern and clean look with a size of 14. And for the body, I used **Source Serif 4** with the size of 11 to make it easier to read. I particularly like this combination of having sans-serif for the headers and serif for the body texts. The headers are also shown in bold for maintaining hierarchy.

### Profile

I eliminated the extra profile section and just combined my portfolio, GitHub, and LinkedIn with the email and phone number I have set at the top. I've added the correct icons for each link as well.

### Skills Section

I divided my skills into different groups so that it is easier to get a clear idea of the tools I'm using for each stack. I also used icons for each tech stack to make them look nicer.

### Projects Section

I only mentioned the most valuable projects I have built alongside the tools used in each project. Instead of using "." to separate the tool's name, I thought of going with "**/**" for a more developer look.

```
TypeScript / React / Node.js / Express
```

#### Project Highlights

I decided to highlight only a few keywords so that they catch the recruiter's eye instantly when scanning through the page. According to a [widely cited study by Ladders](https://www.theladders.com/static/images/basicSite/pdfs/TheLadders-EyeTracking-StudyC2.pdf), recruiters spend an average of 7.4 seconds scanning a resume before deciding whether to read further, so I think it is necessary to catch their attention in as many ways as possible. I only bolded the **tools** and **metrics**. For example:

Reduced unnecessary API calls up to **60%** using **Apollo GraphQL** caching and pagination.

### Education

I combined the education and certifications section into one to avoid wasting space for another header. Every space is valuable in your resume, so make sure to fill them with only what is necessary.

## Conclusion 🧘🏻‍♂️

Overall I'm very satisfied with the final result. I've successfully created a polished resume, which I can use to prepare for my job hunt. Of course resumes don't stay constant, and they'll only evolve with your experiences over time. But maintaining a rigid structure for your resume while keeping the options for flexibility can go a long way.

In the end, it isn't the tools that ultimately matter but the process and stability in your system, though some tools can make your life easier than others. 

My resume is ready. Now comes the harder part...
